Anatomy of a Hydrophone

What's actually inside a research-grade hydrophone — and the chain of parts that turns a whale's click into a number on your screen. Click any part to inspect it.

◎ How to read this page

Two interactive diagrams. First, a cutaway of a research-grade hydrophone — the kind you're building toward (your serious build is a home-made version of exactly this). Second, the signal chain it lives in: where the sound is transduced, amplified, digitised, and processed. Every figure is from a real datasheet — sources are linked.

1Inside a research-grade hydrophone

Tap a numbered part on the cutaway (or the list beside it) to see what it does and why it matters. Working outside → in: a waterproof acoustic window, the piezo element, its preamp, shielding, and the connector and cable that carry the signal up.

    2The signal chain — sound to data

    A hydrophone never works alone. The click is transduced, amplified, digitised, and processed — at distinct points along the chain. Tap a stage to see what happens there (and note: a unit with an integral preamp does its first amplification inside the body, and a "smart" digital hydrophone even moves the ADC in there too).

    ★ Where each thing happens

    Transduce (pressure→voltage) and amplify #1 happen in the hydrophone body, at the element and its integral preamp. Amplify #2 / power is an optional deck stage (only passive units need it). Digitise happens at the recorder's ADC — unless you use a smart digital hydrophone, which digitises inside. Process (spectrograms, detection, TDOA, localization) all happens later, in software on a computer.
